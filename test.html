Osaka Castle :
Osaka Castle, built in 1583 by Toyotomi Hideyoshi, is a iconic symbol of Japan's feudal history. Originally intended as the center of a unified Japan, it was rebuilt by the Tokugawa shogunate in the 1620s. The castle features impressive stone walls, a five-story main keep, and significant cultural and historical significance - 11 Dec 2024.


Japan - Universal Studios :
Universal Studios Japan, located in Osaka, is a premier theme park that combines Universal Studios magic with Japanese efficiency. Opened in 2001, it is the first Universal Studios park in Asia and one of the most visited theme parks globally, attracting 16 million visitors in 2023. The park is divided into 10 zones, including Hollywood, The Wizarding World of Harry Potter, Jurassic Park, Minion Park, and the newly added Super Nintendo World. It features a wide range of attractions, from kid-friendly carousels to thrilling roller coasters and simulations based on popular films and characters like Mario, Harry Potter, and Jurassic Park - 28 Nov 2024


Kyoto - Kinkakuji : 
Kinkakuji, or the Golden Pavilion, is a Zen Buddhist temple in northern Kyoto, famous for its three-story structure with the top two floors covered in gold leaf. Originally the retirement villa of Shogun Ashikaga Yoshimitsu, it was converted into a temple after his death in 1408 and rebuilt in 1955. - 9 Dec 2024


Kyoto - Arashiyama :
Arashiyama, in western Kyoto, is a scenic district known for its natural beauty. Key attractions include the Arashiyama Bamboo Grove, Tenryu-ji Temple, and the iconic Togetsukyo Bridge spanning the Katsura River. The area is famous for its cherry blossoms, autumn foliage, and the Iwatayama Monkey Park, offering a serene Japanese experience - 9 Dec 2024

Kyoto Gion :
Gion is Kyoto's most famous geisha district, located around Shijo Avenue between Yasaka Shrine in the east and the Kamo River in the west. The most popular area of Gion is Hanami-koji Street from Shijo Avenue to Kenninji Temple. - 8 Dec 2024


Kyoto - Nijo Castle :
Nijo Castle, located in Kyoto, Japan, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site showcasing Edo-period architecture and history. Built in 1603, it served as the residence of Tokugawa shoguns. Renowned for its elegant interiors, "nightingale" floors, and beautiful gardens, the castle offers a glimpse into Japan's feudal past and artistic heritage. - 8 Dec 2024


Nara Todaiji :
Todai-ji, a UNESCO World Heritage Site in Nara, Japan, is a monumental Buddhist temple housing the Great Buddha, a colossal bronze statue of Vairocana. Built in 752, it features the world’s largest wooden structure and rich cultural significance, attracting visitors with its architectural grandeur and serene spiritual atmosphere. - 7 Dec 2024


Nara Park :
Nara Park, established in 1880, is a 660-hectare public park in central Nara, Japan. It is renowned for its freely roaming deer, designated as a natural treasure, and houses famous temples like Todaiji, Kofukuji, and Kasuga Taisha. The park also features scenic areas like Mt. Wakakusa and Sagi-ike Pond - 7 Dec 2024


Kyoto - Fushimi Inari Taisha :
Fushimi Inari Taisha, located in Kyoto, Japan, is a famous Shinto shrine dedicated to Inari, the deity of rice and prosperity. Known for its iconic thousands of red torii gates forming picturesque trails, the shrine attracts visitors seeking blessings and enjoying scenic hikes through its sacred forested mountains. - 6 Dec 2024


Kyoto - Kiyomizudera Temple :
Kiyomizudera Temple, a UNESCO World Heritage Site in Kyoto, Japan, is renowned for its stunning wooden stage overlooking cherry blossoms and maple trees. Founded in 778, this historic Buddhist temple offers breathtaking views, sacred shrines, and the iconic Otowa Waterfall, symbolizing purity, wisdom, and health, attracting visitors worldwide. - 6 Dec 2024


Tokyo Disney Land :
Tokyo Disneyland, located in Urayasu, Japan, is a magical theme park that opened in 1983 as the first Disney park outside the United States. Inspired by the original Disneyland in California, it features seven themed lands: World Bazaar, Adventureland, Westernland, Fantasyland, Tomorrowland, Critter Country, and Toontown. Known for its iconic Cinderella Castle, beloved Disney characters, and family-friendly atmosphere, the park offers exciting attractions like Space Mountain, Pirates of the Caribbean, and It's a Small World. With enchanting parades, spectacular fireworks, and a variety of dining and shopping experiences, Tokyo Disneyland delivers unforgettable fun and wonder for visitors of all ages. - 4 Dec 2024

Tokyo Disney Sea :
Tokyo Disney Sea is a unique Disney theme park located in Urayasu, Japan, near Tokyo. Opened in 2001, it blends nautical themes with Disney magic, offering seven themed ports of call: Mediterranean Harbor, American Waterfront, Port Discovery, Lost River Delta, Arabian Coast, Mermaid Lagoon, and Mysterious Island. Known for its stunning design, immersive storytelling, and attractions like Journey to the Center of the Earth and Soaring: Fantastic Flight, it caters to all ages. With exquisite dining, captivating shows, and meticulous attention to detail, Tokyo DisneySea provides a one-of-a-kind experience, often hailed as Disney’s most beautiful and innovative park worldwide. - 3 Dec 2024


Ehime is located in the northwest of Shikoku, an island located southwest of Japan’s main island of Honshu, is renowned for its stunning natural beauty, including the Seto Inland Sea and lush mountains. Matsuyama airport offers easy access from Osaka and Tokyo. The Shimanami Kaido connects Ehime to Hiroshima via islands on the Seto Inland Sea. Famous for Dogo Onsen, one of Japan’s oldest hot springs, it also boasts historic castles, citrus orchards, and the Shimanami Kaido cycling route. Ehime offers a serene blend of tradition, outdoor adventure, and local culinary delights.
